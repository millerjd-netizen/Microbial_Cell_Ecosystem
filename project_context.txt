PROJECT TITLE
"Creating Our Own Microbial Cell Ecosystem: Division of Labor & Auxotrophic
Cross-Feeding in an MFC Co-culture"
================================================================================

BIOLOGICAL FOUNDATION
---------------------
We are designing a synthetic co-culture system involving two organisms:

  1. Escherichia coli (E. coli)
     - Role: Scavenger / Oxygen remover / Acetate provider
     - Removes oxygen from the system (creating the anaerobic condition Geobacter needs)
     - Produces and provides acetate as a carbon/energy source for Geobacter
     - Is auxotrophic for tryptophan — it CANNOT synthesize tryptophan on its own,
       so it depends on Geobacter to survive

  2. Geobacter (G. sulfurreducens + G. metallireducens)
     - Role: Electricity generator
     - Consumes acetate and generates electricity via extracellular electron transfer (EET)
     - Secretes tryptophan, which E. coli needs to survive
     - Enhanced with OmcZ outer-membrane cytochrome boosters to maximize EET

CORE CONCEPT — Division of Labor (DoL) + Auxotrophic Cross-Feeding
  - Neither organism can survive without the other
  - E. coli and Geobacter exchange metabolites (acetate/tryptophan), creating obligate mutualism
  - This enforces stable cooperation and prevents cheater mutants from dominating
  - The system operates inside a Microbial Fuel Cell (MFC), converting metabolic activity
    into measurable electrical current

WHY THIS IS INTERESTING
  - Evolutionary stability: cheaters starve without partner amino acids
  - Bioelectricity: Geobacter's electron transfer to an electrode generates real electricity
  - Synthetic biology: we are engineering the dependency — it doesn't exist in nature this way
  - Computational design: we use formal specification languages (KNOX/GOLDBAR) and
    stochastic simulation (TinkerCell) to design and validate these circuits

GENETIC CIRCUIT DESIGN (via KNOX/GOLDBAR DSL)
================================================================================

The circuit designs are written in KNOX/GOLDBAR specification language.
There are 8 total circuits across two organisms:

--- GEOBACTER CIRCUITS ---

  StrainA (G. sulfurreducens):
    Specification: "Ptrc then RBS1 then trpB then Term1"
    Parts: promoter Ptrc, RBS1, CDS trpB, terminator Term1
    Function: Drives tryptophan biosynthesis (trpB gene) for secretion to E. coli

  StrainB (G. metallireducens):
    Specification: "PnifH then RBS2 then leuA then Term2"
    Parts: promoter PnifH, RBS2, CDS leuA, terminator Term2
    Function: Drives leucine biosynthesis for cross-feeding

  KillSwitchStrainA:
    Specification: "Pconst then RBS1 then mazE then Term1 then Prep then RBS1 then mazF then Term1"
    Parts: promoters Pconst + Prep, RBS1, CDS mazE (antitoxin) + mazF (toxin), Term1
    Function: MazEF toxin-antitoxin kill switch — if cross-feeding breaks down, mazF kills the cell

  KillSwitchStrainB:
    Specification: "Pconst then RBS2 then mazE then Term2 then Prep then RBS2 then mazF then Term2"
    Parts: same architecture as KillSwitchStrainA but with RBS2/Term2 for G. metallireducens

  OmcZBoosterStrainA:
    Specification: "PomcZ then RBS1 then omcZ then Term1"
    Parts: promoter PomcZ, RBS1, CDS omcZ, Term1
    Function: Overexpresses OmcZ cytochrome to boost extracellular electron transfer → more electricity

  OmcZBoosterStrainB:
    Specification: "PomcZ then RBS2 then omcZ then Term2"
    Function: Same booster for G. metallireducens strain

--- E. COLI CIRCUITS ---

  EcoliLcircuit:
    Specification: "leuB then leuC then leuD"
    Parts CDS: leuA, leuB, leuC, leuD
    Function: Leucine biosynthesis pathway — produces leucine for cross-feeding to Geobacter

  EcoliKcircuit:
    Specification: "lysC then argD"
    Parts CDS: lysA, lysC, argD
    Function: Lysine biosynthesis pathway — produces lysine for cross-feeding

TOTAL: 8 circuits (6 Geobacter, 2 E. coli)

================================================================================
COMPUTATIONAL METHODS USED
================================================================================

1. KNOX / GOLDBAR (Circuit Design & Specification)
   - KNOX is a formal DSL (Domain-Specific Language) for specifying genetic circuits
   - GOLDBAR enumerates all valid circuit assemblies given the KNOX specification
   - Used to enumerate and validate 6 Geobacter circuit designs
   - Produces a structured list of all biologically valid part combinations

2. TinkerCell (Stochastic Simulation)
   - Used to simulate evolutionary dynamics of the co-culture system
   - Inputs: KNOX CSV output → TinkerCell
   - Four simulation scenarios modeled:
       Scenario 1 — Monoculture baseline:
         Single engineered strain alone. Cheater mutants dominate; engineered function lost.
       Scenario 2 — Two-strain DoL only (no amino acid dependency):
         Division of labor exists but cooperation is unstable; cheaters still viable.
       Scenario 3 — DoL + Auxotrophic cross-feeding:
         Cheaters starve without their partner's amino acid; cooperation enforced by starvation.
       Scenario 4 — Full consortium (Kill switch + OmcZ booster):
         Cheaters killed by MazF toxin, current maximized by OmcZ; maximum evolutionary stability.
   - Output: evolutionary stability comparison plots, ODE-based system dynamics

3. recommend.py (Python Script — Custom Tool)
   - A pure-Python recommendation engine for circuit selection
   - Takes user goals/priorities as input (e.g., maximize electricity, maximize stability,
     minimize toxicity) and scores/ranks circuits accordingly
   - Currently: sorts circuits by weighted scoring across 4 categories
   - STATUS: Needs more computational substance before April 22nd (see section below)

================================================================================
THE recommend.py SCRIPT — WHAT IT DOES & WHAT NEEDS TO BE ADDED
================================================================================

CURRENT STATE
  recommend.py accepts user priority weights and scores the 18 circuits
  (this seems to have expanded from 8 to ~18 total design variants) against 4 categories.
  It ranks circuits by weighted score and returns the top recommendations.
  This is essentially a weighted sort — it works but lacks computational depth.

THE PROBLEM
  A weighted sort is not defensible as a class project by itself. It does not demonstrate
  computational complexity, biological reasoning, or algorithmic thinking. To meet the
  "Appropriate Complexity" requirement (10 pts) and "Detailed Functionality" (30 pts)
  on the grading rubric, recommend.py needs more substance.

FOUR ADDITIONS PLANNED (Pure Python, no external libraries, feasible by April 22):

  1. COMPATIBILITY CHECKING
     - E. coli circuits and Geobacter circuits use different RBS/promoter systems
       (e.g., RBS1 vs RBS2, Ptrc vs PnifH vs Pconst)
     - Not all circuits can be combined biologically
     - The script should detect when top-recommended circuits come from incompatible
       organisms or use conflicting genetic parts, and warn the user
     - Requires conditional logic and part-system mapping — not just sorting

  2. CIRCUIT COMBINATION MODE
     - Instead of recommending a single circuit, recommend a PAIR:
       one E. coli circuit + one Geobacter circuit that together maximize the user's goals
     - This requires iterating over all (E. coli circuit × Geobacter circuit) pairs
     - With 18 circuits, this is an 18×18 = 324 pair combinatorial search space
     - This is genuinely interesting computationally: it's an optimization over pairs,
       not just a sort over individuals

  3. TRADEOFF VISUALIZATION IN THE TERMINAL
     - Print an ASCII bar chart or comparison table showing each top circuit's scores
       across all 4 categories side by side
     - Forces the user to see tradeoffs, not just blindly accept the #1 recommendation
     - Example format:
         Circuit         | Electricity | Stability | Toxicity | Yield
         ----------------------------------------------------------------
         OmcZBoosterA    | ████████    | █████     | ██       | ██████
         StrainA_Ptrc    | █████       | ████████  | ████     | ████
     - Requires building the ASCII rendering logic from scratch in Python

  4. CONFLICT DETECTION FROM KNOX SPEC STRINGS
     - Parse the knox_spec string field for each circuit
     - Check whether two circuits in a recommended pair share the same promoter
       (e.g., both use Ptrc, which would cause expression interference in the same cell)
     - This directly uses the biological specification data in a computational way
     - Requires string parsing and cross-circuit comparison logic
