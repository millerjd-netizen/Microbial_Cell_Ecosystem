PAGE 2 — PROJECT SUMMARY
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
Designing a co-culture system where E. coli and Geobacter depend on each other
through Division of Labor (DoL) and amino acid cross-feeding (they cannot survive
without each other).

  - E. coli will support the Geobacter by removing oxygen and providing acetate.
  - Geobacter generates the electricity from the acetate and secretes tryptophan,
    which the E. coli needs.
  - Using KNOX/GOLDBAR to design the valid genetic circuits and Tinkercell to see
    how stable the system is.

KEY CONCEPTS:
  Division of Labor:
    Each strain has one role; no organism does it all.

  Auxotrophic Cross-Feeding:
    Amino acid dependency enforces cooperation.

  Knox + Tinkercell:
    DSL circuit design + stochastic simulation.

PAGE 5 — PROGRESS (3/3): SIMULATION WITH TINKERCELL
================================================================================

PIPELINE:
  Knox CSV → Tinkercell Simulations → Evolutionary Dynamics Model → Comparison Plots

SIMULATION SCENARIOS:

  Scenario 1 — Monoculture Baseline:
    Single engineered strain alone.
    Result: Cheater mutants dominate, engineered function lost.

  Scenario 2 — Two-Strain DoL Only:
    Division of labor without auxotrophy.
    Result: Cooperation unstable, cheaters still viable.

  Scenario 3 — DoL + Auxotrophic Cross-Feeding:
    Cheaters starve without partner amino acid.
    Result: Cooperative strains enforced by starvation.

  Scenario 4 — Full Consortium + Kill Switch + OmcZ Booster:
    Cheaters killed by MazF toxin, current maximized by OmcZ.
    Result: Maximum evolutionary stability.
